What we sell (one sentence)

Kostas is an AI voice receptionist for barbershops — the shop forwards their phone line to us, Kostas answers in 7 languages, takes the booking, and drops it into the owner's calendar. We bill flat monthly by included voice minutes, in 4 tiers. No overage, no top-ups, no per-location fee.

Copy guardrails (instructions to your Claude)

Do

  • Lead with calls answered, bookings made, not "minutes."
  • Name tiers by volume (Light / Standard / Busy / Heavy) so owners self-match.
  • Frame the hard cap as a feature ("flat bill, no surprises").
  • Mention the 7 languages — real wedge in Athens / tourist zones.
  • Badge Standard = Most popular, Heavy = Best value.

Don't

  • Don't mention "unlimited locations" or "multi-shop support" — locations aren't a billable dimension, mentioning it invents a limit-shaped worry.
  • Don't promise overage, top-ups, or pay-as-you-go. The cap is deliberate.
  • Don't show a per-call price. We sell minutes.
  • Don't write "up to 100 minutes" — write "100 min/month". "Up to" reads like a cap the customer won't actually hit.
  • Don't invent your own discount — promos are in the canonical JSON, only use those codes.
